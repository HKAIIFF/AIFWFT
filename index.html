THE CHALLENGE

The "Last Mile" of Spatial Computing

Latency Kills Immersion

Traditional cloud rendering adds >100ms latency. AR/VR needs <20ms to prevent motion sickness.

Centralized Costs

Deploying dedicated edge servers is too expensive for single apps. Infrastructure must be shared.

THE SOLUTION

Distributed Edge Fog

AIF.WTF aggregates consumer GPUs (RTX 3060+) and commercial servers into a unified fog network. Content is cached physically close to users in commercial districts, enabling instant rendering and interactions.

  • H3 Hexagonal Indexing
  • TEE Privacy Computing
  • Dynamic Resource Allocation
Architecture

Cloud-Fog-Edge Trinity

A robust three-layer architecture designed for the high-bandwidth, low-latency demands of the Metaverse.

404.world (Cloud)

Content generation hub where AI creates infinite 3D assets and narratives. Handles heavy lifting of content creation and long-term storage.

AIF Nodes (Fog)

The core innovation. Distributed nodes handle real-time rendering, physics simulation, and TEE-based privacy computing with sub-20ms latency.

404GLASS (Edge)

Lightweight hardware (under 180g) focused on sensing and display. Heavy compute is offloaded to the Fog layer, enabling all-day battery life.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the return on staking?

Returns vary by Tier and location demand. Generally, Tier S/A nodes in high-traffic areas earn 12-24% APY plus usage fees.

Can I use my own hardware?

Yes. You can join via "BYO Device" if your hardware meets the minimum specs (RTX 3060 or equivalent). You still need to stake the required AIF.

Why is there a lock-up period?

To ensure network stability and reliability for enterprise clients, nodes must commit to a 12-month service period.
